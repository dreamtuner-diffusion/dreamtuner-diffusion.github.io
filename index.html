Abstract

Large diffusion-based models have demonstrated impressive capabilities in text-to-image generation, and are expected to lend themselves well to personalized applications which require the generation of customized concepts with one or a small number of reference images. However, existing fine-tuning based subject-driven image generation methods necessitate a delicate balance between subject learning and the maintenance of the generation capabilities of pretrained models. Furthermore, other methods based on additional image encoders tend to lose important details of the reference image due to compression. To address these issues, we propose DreamTurner, a new refined reference-based fine-tuning and inference method. It is worth noting that the self-attention layers within pretrained text-to-image models naturally perform spatial contextual association functions. We propose a novel subject-attention mechanism which refines the reference. Specifically, the reference features are extracted from the reference image using pretrained text-to-image models which share a common distribution with the generating image features. Subsequently, the reference features are concatenated with the generating image features, where they are used as the key and value for the self-attention layers. In order to solve the issue of the background of the reference image impacting the generated image, we introduce an additional mask strategy. It is worth emphasizing that subject-attention is an elegant, effective, and training-free method of maintaining the detailed features of customized concepts, which can be used as a plug-and-play solution during inference. Furthermore, the performance of DreamTurner in subject-driven image generation, controlled by text or other conditions such as pose, has been markedly improved through the utilization of subject-attention and a number of other fine-tuning techniques.

Approach

Our proposed method uses one or very few images of a subject as input (typically a single image suffices for most subjects). Initially, DreamBooth is utilized to learn a word embedding [S*] that represents the subject. This finetunes the pre-trained text-to-image model, employing a custom dataset of {'a photo of [class word] [S*]'-reference images} and {'a photo of [class word]'-regular images}. For refined regular image generation, we incorporated a novel subject attention mechanism alongside some image captioning based techniques. The subject attention relies on self-attention layers to furnish fine-grained spatial reference during generation. During the inference stage, a model which has undergone fine-tuning is utilized to generate several images of the subject. The subject attention mechanism with mask and weight strategy is then applied to maintain the essential details of the generated images.



Our proposed method utilizes subject attention, which is based on original self-attention layers within pre-trained text-to-image models (in this case, Stable Diffusion models). The process involves keeping the generating image features at timestep t as the query, while using the concatenation of generating image features and reference image features as the key and value. To extract reference image features, we employ a shared Diffusion U-Net to apply diffusion noise to the reference image. Salient Object Detection (SOD) is then used to create a foreground mask, protecting the generating image from the reference image's background. By adopting the mask as the attention mask in subject attention, we introduce a weight strategy to adjust the scale of the reference image's impact.

Results of Text-Controlled Anime Characters Driven Image Generation

Our results display the output of text-controlled subject-driven image generation focused on anime characters. DreamTuner fine-tuning was performed using only a single image. Both local editing (such as expression editing in the first line) and global editing (including scene and action editing in the subsequent five lines) were performed, resulting in highly detailed images even with complex text inputs. Notably, the images maintained the details of the reference images accurately.

Results of Text-Controlled Natural Images Driven Image Generation

Our method is evaluated on the DreamBooth dataset, where one image of each subject is utilized as the reference image. Through the use of subject attention, a refined reference is generated, which enables DreamTuner to successfully produce high-fidelity images that are consistent with the textual input, while also retaining crucial subject details, including but not limited to, white stripes on puppy's head, logos on bag, patterns and texts on can.
